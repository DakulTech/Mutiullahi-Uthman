I hold a degree in Agricultural Economics and Extension from Ladoke Akintola University of Technology (LAUTECH). You might wonder — how does an agricultural graduate end up in Software Engineering?

It happened as a challenge. After years of cryptocurrency trading, COVID-era ambition sparked an idea — launch my own crypto token. The whitepaper was drafted. The vision was mapped.

The agency I contacted quoted a price I simply couldn't afford. That one moment killed the idea — and lit a fire that hasn't gone out since.

From that day I made a promise: learn to write code, build my own projects, use software engineering to solve real problems. And here I am — living exactly that promise.
